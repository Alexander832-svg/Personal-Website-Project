Video games are defined as digital or electronic games that can be interacted with via an input device to produce digital responses on the device being used. They encompass various genres, including action, adventure, and role-playing, and play a significant role in the entertainment industry.


Video games are categorized into genres based on their core gameplay mechanics and objectives, with common types including Action (fast-paced, combat-focused), Adventure (story-driven, puzzle-solving), Role-Playing (character development, fantasy worlds), Simulation (real-world activity replication), Strategy (planning, resource management), Sports (real-world sports simulation), Puzzle, and Shooter (first-person or third-person combat).
